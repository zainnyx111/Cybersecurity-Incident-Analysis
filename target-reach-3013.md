# Target Corporation Breach (2013)

## 1. Incident Overview
In late 2013, Target suffered a breach exposing the financial and personal information of millions of customers. The breach occurred during the holiday season and was publicly disclosed in December 2013. Approximately 110 million customers were affected.

## 2. Compromised Assets
- Customer payment card information (numbers, CVV, expiry dates)  
- Names, emails, phone numbers, mailing addresses  

**Scope:** Millions of credit/debit cards and personal information, enabling fraud and identity theft.

## 3. Attack Vector
- Stolen credentials from a third-party HVAC vendor  
- Malware deployed on POS terminals  
- Weak network segmentation and lack of vendor access controls  

## 4. Threat Elimination Strategy
1. **Containment:** Disconnect affected POS systems and isolate networks  
2. **Credential Management:** Reset vendor/admin accounts, enforce MFA  
3. **Malware Removal:** Identify and remove POS malware  
4. **Incident Response:** Notify affected customers and banks, forensic investigation  

## 5. Threat Prevention Strategy
- Strong vendor access controls and MFA  
- Network segmentation for sensitive systems  
- Real-time monitoring of POS and anomalous traffic  
- Employee/vendor cybersecurity awareness  
- End-to-end payment card encryption and regular penetration testing
